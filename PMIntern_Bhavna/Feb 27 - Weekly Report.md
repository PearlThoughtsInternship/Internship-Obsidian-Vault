**Project:** Schedula – Salon Booking App  
**Week:** 23 February – 27 February 2026

# Monday – 23 Feb

## Topic: Security, Privacy, Uptime & SLAs + Personal Productivity for PMs

Objective

Understand how non-functional requirements (security, privacy, uptime, SLAs) impact scope, cost, and timelines.

## Key Learnings

### 1️. Non-Functional Requirements Impact Delivery

Even a simple salon booking app like **Schedula** handles:

- Customer phone numbers
    
- Appointment history
    
- Revenue data
    
- Business-critical workflows
    

We discussed how data leaks or downtime during peak weekends can severely impact business trust and revenue.


## 2️.  Security & Privacy Fundamentals for PMs

Learned core principles:

- **Confidentiality** – Only authorized users access data
    
- **Integrity** – Data cannot be modified without trace
    
- **Availability** – System must remain accessible
    

Clarified difference:

- Security = Lock on door
    
- Privacy = Who is allowed inside
    

Understood how stronger security increases:

- Development complexity
    
- Infrastructure cost
    
- Testing effort
    
- Delivery timeline


## 3️. SLA & Uptime Impact

Discussed how increasing uptime from 98% to 99.9% requires:

- Better cloud infrastructure
    
- Monitoring tools
    
- Backup systems
    
- On-call engineers
    

Key Insight:  
Higher SLA commitments directly increase budget and team requirements.

## 4️. Risk & Compliance Impact on Scope

Compared:

Basic Feature → Secure Feature

Example:

- Basic: Show appointment list
    
- Secure: Role-based access + logs + restrictions
    

Learned that compliance adds hidden complexity to features.

## 5️. Personal Productivity for PMs

Covered:

### ✅ Personal Kanban

- Visualize all tasks
    
- Limit WIP to 3
    

### ✅ Calendar Blocking

- Focus blocks (60–90 min)
    
- Admin blocks
    
- Buffer time
    

### ✅ Decision Logs

Structured format to document key decisions to prevent repeated debates.

### ✅ Burnout Prevention

- Fixed Slack checking times
    
- End-of-day shutdown routine
    
- Clear work boundaries

# Wednesday – 25 Feb

## Topic: Discovery Workshops & Requirement Sessions

### Objective

Prevent scope confusion through structured workshops.

## Key Learnings

### 1️.  Why Workshops Replace Emails

Compared:  
5-day email chain vs 1-hour structured workshop.

Understood that workshops:

- Clarify scope
    
- Align stakeholders
    
- Prevent scope creep
    
- Reduce rework

## 2️. 60-Minute Workshop Framework

Structured flow:

0–5 min → Ground rules  
5–15 min → Context setting  
15–25 min → Silent brainstorming  
25–35 min → Round robin sharing  
35–50 min → MoSCoW prioritization  
50–60 min → Alignment & action items

## 3️. MoSCoW Prioritization (Schedula Example)

Must Have:

- Booking
    
- Calendar
    
- SMS
    

Should Have:

- Staff scheduling
    

Could Have:

- Reports
    

Won’t Have:

- Payments
    
- Google sync
