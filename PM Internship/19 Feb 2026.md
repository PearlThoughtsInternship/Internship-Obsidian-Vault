## üéØ Part 1: Understanding Scope Creep (10 minutes)

### What is Scope Creep?

**Definition:** The uncontrolled expansion of project scope without adjustments to time, cost, or resources.

## How Scope Creep Happens (Schedula Examples)

|Week|Request|Sounds Harmless?|Actual Impact|
|---|---|---|---|
|2|"Can the button be blue instead of green?"|‚úì Yes|15 min change|
|3|"Can we add customer photos?"|‚úì Yes|+8 hours (storage, upload, display)|
|4|"Can customers book via WhatsApp?"|‚úì Yes|+40 hours (new integration)|
|5|"Can we add loyalty points system?"|‚úì Yes|+80 hours (new database, logic, UI)|

**Cumulative Impact:** +128 hours = **3+ weeks delay** from "small requests"

> [!warning] The Silent Killer  
> Scope creep doesn't announce itself. It accumulates through:
> 
> - "Quick changes" that compound
>     
> - "While you're at it" additions
>     
> - Ambiguous requirements interpreted broadly
>     
> - Gold-plating (team adding unrequested features)
>

## üîÑ Part 2: The 5-Step Change Control Process (15 minutes)

## Professional Change Management Flow

text

`flowchart TD   A[Change Request Received] --> B{Step 1: Log Request}  B --> C[Step 2: Impact Assessment]  C --> D[Step 3: Generate Options]  D --> E[Step 4: Present Trade-offs]  E --> F{Step 5: Decision}  F -->|Approved| G[Update Plan & Communicate]  F -->|Rejected| H[Document & Explain Why]  F -->|Deferred| I[Add to Phase 2 Backlog]  style A fill:#E3F2FD  style F fill:#FFF3E0  style G fill:#C8E6C9  style H fill:#FFCDD2  style I fill:#F0F4C3`



## Step-by-Step Breakdown

## **Step 1: Log the Request**

Create formal record immediately:

text

`## Change Request #CR-012 **Date:** 2026-02-19 **Requestor:** Salon Owner (Client) **Request:** "Add WhatsApp booking integration" **Priority:** High (per client) **Status:** Under Review`

## **Step 2: Impact Assessment**

Analyze using three dimensions:

text

`graph TD   A[Change Request:<br/>WhatsApp Integration] --> B[Scope Impact]  A --> C[Time Impact]  A --> D[Cost Impact]  B --> B1[+5 story points<br/>New API integration<br/>New UI screens]  C --> C1[+1 week dev<br/>+3 days testing<br/>Delays launch]  D --> D1[+‚Çπ25,000 dev<br/>+‚Çπ5,000 WhatsApp API<br/>Monthly costs]  style A fill:#4ECDC4  style B fill:#FFE66D  style C fill:#FF6B6B  style D fill:#95E1D3`

**Schedula WhatsApp Integration Analysis:**

|Dimension|Current Plan|With Change|Delta|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Scope**|18 story points|23 story points|+5 pts (+28%)|
|**Timeline**|Launch Feb 28|Launch Mar 7|+7 days|
|**Cost**|‚Çπ8,00,000|‚Çπ8,30,000|+‚Çπ30,000|
|**Team Load**|At capacity|128% capacity|Overload risk|

## **Step 3: Generate Options**

Never present just "Yes" or "No". Always give **3 options**:

> [!example] Schedula WhatsApp Options
> 
> **Option A: Accept & Delay Launch**
> 
> - ‚úÖ Client gets WhatsApp feature
>     
> - ‚ùå Launch delayed 1 week
>     
> - ‚ùå Marketing campaign timing affected
>     
> - Cost: +‚Çπ30,000
>     
> 
> **Option B: Defer to Phase 2**
> 
> - ‚úÖ Launch on time (Feb 28)
>     
> - ‚úÖ Budget unchanged
>     
> - ‚ùå No WhatsApp at launch
>     
> - ‚úÖ Can promote "WhatsApp coming soon"
>     
> - Timeline: Add in March update
>     
> 
> **Option C: Swap Features**
> 
> - ‚úÖ Launch on time
>     
> - Add WhatsApp, remove "Monthly Reports" feature
>     
> - Same scope (23 pts ‚Üí 18 pts after removal)
>     
> - ‚ùå Lose analytics capability temporarily
>     

## **Step 4: Present Trade-offs (The Iron Triangle)**

Visualize the constraint relationship:

text

`graph TD   A[Good] --> B[Fast]  A --> C[Cheap]  B --> C  B -.->|Pick 2| D[High Quality<br/>+ On Time<br/>= Expensive]  A -.->|Pick 2| E[High Quality<br/>+ Cheap<br/>= Slow]  C -.->|Pick 2| F[Fast<br/>+ Cheap<br/>= Low Quality]  style A fill:#4ECDC4  style B fill:#FF6B6B  style C fill:#95E1D3`

**Present to client using this script:**

> "We can absolutely add WhatsApp booking. To maintain quality, we need to adjust either:
> 
> 1. **Timeline** ‚Äì Push launch to March 7 (+1 week)
>     
> 2. **Scope** ‚Äì Remove Monthly Reports feature to make room
>     
> 3. **Budget** ‚Äì Add ‚Çπ30,000 for overtime development
>     
> 
> Which constraint are you most flexible on?"

## **Step 5: Document Decision**

Whatever the outcome, record it:

text

`## Change Request #CR-012 - DECISION **Date:** 2026-02-19 **Decision:** Option B - Defer to Phase 2 **Decided By:** Client Stakeholder (Salon Owner) **Rationale:** - Launch timing critical for seasonal business (wedding season) - WhatsApp nice-to-have but not blocking launch - Will promote as "Coming in March update" **Actions:** - [ ] Update roadmap with Phase 2 WhatsApp feature - [ ] Communicate decision to dev team - [ ] Add to marketing messaging: "WhatsApp integration coming soon" - [ ] Create Phase 2 epic in Jira **Communication Sent:** Yes (email to stakeholders 2026-02-19 14:30)`

## Prevention Strategies

text

`mindmap   root((Scope Creep<br/>Prevention))    Clear Requirements      Written user stories      Acceptance criteria      Definition of Done    Change Control Process      Formal request form      Impact assessment      Stakeholder approval    Regular Communication      Weekly status updates      Demo early and often      Manage expectations    Strong Project Charter      Defined scope boundaries      Out-of-scope items listed      Sign-off from stakeholders`

> - ‚ùå Payment processing (Phase 2)

> - ‚ùå Video call consultation booking

## Saying "No" Professionally

**Bad PM Response:**

> "No, we can't do that. It's not in scope."

**Good PM Response:**

> "That's a great idea for improving customer engagement. Adding WhatsApp would require adjusting our timeline or scope. Let me present three options so we can make the best decision together. When would be a good time to review the trade-offs?"

**Your Task (10 minutes):**

## üìö Key Takeaways

> [!summary] Today's Core Lessons
> 
> 1. **Scope creep kills projects silently** through accumulated "small" changes
>     
> 2. **5-step change control process** is non-negotiable:
>     
>     - Log ‚Üí Assess ‚Üí Options ‚Üí Trade-offs ‚Üí Decide
>         
> 3. **Never present just Yes/No** ‚Äì always give 3 options with trade-offs
>     
> 4. **Iron Triangle is your friend** ‚Äì visually show that changing one dimension affects others
>     
> 5. **Saying "No" professionally** means showing data and offering alternatives
>     
> 6. **Prevention beats cure** ‚Äì clear scope boundaries in project charter prevent most issues
>     
> 7. **Every change request deserves documentation** ‚Äì future you (and auditors) will thank you
>
